Title: Quick and Easy Spicy Tomato Ricotta Pasta with Sundried Tomatoes

Ingredients:
- 1 pound of pasta
- 2 cups cherry tomatoes, halved
- 4 cloves of garlic, minced
- 1/4 cup olive oil
- 1 cup ricotta cheese
- 1/4 cup sundried tomatoes, chopped
- Pinch of red pepper flakes (optional)
- Salt and pepper to taste
- Parmesan cheese, grated (optional)

Overview: This quick and easy pasta dish is perfect for a weeknight dinner. Cherry tomatoes and garlic are sautéed in olive oil and then mixed with creamy ricotta cheese and sundried tomatoes for a delicious and satisfying sauce. A pinch of red pepper flakes adds some spiciness to the dish. The dish comes together in under an hour and is sure to please the whole family.

Tips:
- Be sure not to overcook the pasta, as it will continue to cook when mixed with the sauce.
- If the ricotta cheese is too thick, add a splash of pasta water to thin it out.
- If you prefer a spicier dish, add a pinch of red pepper flakes or sliced chili peppers to the tomato sauce.

Instructions:
1. Cook the pasta according to the package instructions until al dente.
2. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat.
3. Add the garlic and sauté for 1-2 minutes, until fragrant.
4. Add the cherry tomatoes to the skillet and cook for 5-7 minutes, until they start to soften and the skins begin to wrinkle.
5. Add the sundried tomatoes and red pepper flakes (if using) to the skillet and stir to combine.
6. Season the tomato mixture with salt and pepper to taste.
7. Drain the pasta and add it to the skillet with the tomato mixture.
8. Add the ricotta cheese to the skillet and stir until the cheese is melted and well combined with the tomato sauce.
9. Serve hot, sprinkled with grated Parmesan cheese, if desired.
