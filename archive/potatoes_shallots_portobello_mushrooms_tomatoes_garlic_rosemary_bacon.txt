Ingredients:
- 4 large potatoes, peeled and sliced
- 4 shallots, peeled and sliced
- 4 portobello mushrooms, sliced
- 2 medium tomatoes, diced
- 4 cloves of garlic, minced
- 2 sprigs of fresh rosemary, finely chopped
- 6 strips of bacon, sliced
- 1 tsp. of paprika
- Salt and black pepper to taste
- 2 tbsp. of olive oil
- 2 tbsp. of hot sauce

Instructions:

1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).

2. In a large bowl, mix the potatoes, shallots, portobello mushrooms, and tomatoes together.

3. In a skillet, cook the bacon pieces over medium heat until crispy. Remove and set aside on a paper towel-lined plate.

4. Using the same skillet, add the garlic, rosemary, paprika, salt, and black pepper. Cook the mixture for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.

5. Add the hot sauce and stir until everything is well combined.

6. Pour the garlic and hot sauce mixture over the potato mixture and toss until everything is well coated.

7. Transfer the mixture to a 9x13 inch baking dish and spread it out evenly.

8. Sprinkle the bacon pieces over the top of the potato mixture.

9. Cover the dish with aluminum foil and bake for 30 minutes.

10. Remove the aluminum foil and continue baking for another 15-20 minutes until the potatoes are tender and the bacon is crispy.

Note: You will also need to buy olive oil for this recipe.
